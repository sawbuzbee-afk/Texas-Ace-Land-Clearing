Land Clearing in Tomball, TX

Tomball sits at the southwest edge of our service area, right where Montgomery County meets Harris County — and it's one of the most active zones for rural-to-residential land conversion in the greater Houston region. Acreage that has been in families for generations is being subdivided, sold and built on at a pace that keeps contractors busy year-round. When those new owners call us, the job is usually one of two things: clearing a previously wooded or brushy lot to build a new home, or reclaiming pasture land that has given itself back to woody vegetation over the years of neglect.

The soils around Tomball are a genuine advantage for land clearing work. Sandy loam is common across much of the area — it drains well, doesn't compact and swell the way black clay does, and allows our CAT D4K2 Dozer and CAT 299D2 Track Loader to work efficiently without fighting the ground. We rarely deal with the ground-sticking, equipment-bogging conditions that slow jobs down on the heavier soils further west. That said, low areas near creek drainages can hold moisture and get soft, so we time our work and choose equipment carefully to avoid tearing up the ground unnecessarily.

Vegetation on Tomball properties is a familiar mix: Chinese tallow, yaupon holly, water oak, post oak and the occasional wax myrtle thicket. Pasture reclamation is a big job category here — fields that were once productive hay ground or horse pasture have been overtaken by volunteer tree growth and heavy brush, and the owners want that land back in productive shape. Our Hydro-Ax mulcher handles the brush and smaller trees in a single pass, leaving wood chips that break down into the soil rather than piles of debris that need hauling. We can often combine a Tomball job with other projects in Magnolia or Cypress on the same day, which helps keep mobilization costs efficient for jobs on the smaller end. Call us — the drive from Montgomery is straightforward and we're out there regularly.
